The Garage

a runway for what we'll actually wear

san francisco spring 2026

the future isn't something you put on for a demo. it's what you wear every day.

this is a runway for garments where technology belongs in the silhouette — not bolted on, not announced, just there. pieces that make you want to be seen in them.

  1. no nostalgia. no retrofuturism. no decade references. no costume logic.
  2. a new silhouette language — not trend-adjacent.
  3. wearable in daylight, not just on stage.
  4. the piece must create desire, not curiosity.
  5. beauty first: proportion, restraint, finish.
  6. tech must belong in the look — never the point.
  7. if it reads as a demo, it's out.
  8. if it needs explanation, it's not ready.
  9. it should make someone want to be seen in it.
